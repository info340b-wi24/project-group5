Group 5 Project Proposal: Seattle Hangout Guide

By: Rima Rafiq, Michelle Kim, Pushkar Rimmalapudi, and Hannah Lam

Problem Description

Our project essentially aims to provide a comprehensive guide for residents and tourists to find activities to do in the Seattle area. As students in Seattle, we commonly hear students talking about how they want activities to do, but that it’s hard to discover events just by word of mouth. Our topic aims to address the issue of finding new ways to spend time in Seattle, as well as helps restaurants and activities gain more exposure and business. One of the main problems related to our topic is that there is no central location for information related to activities in Seattle. In terms of previous solutions, there is currently one app on the market called Stranger Things to Do Seattle. This app claims to offer events calendar on what to do in Seattle and daily suggestions, curated by local experts. However, there are many issues with this existing attempt. It’s currently not being updated anymore, has poor navigation with redirecting from the app to the website, and the interface isn’t user friendly. With our project, we strive to create an app that would improve the lives of Seattle residents by encouraging them to get out of the house more through exciting events, beautiful hikes, and new restaurants. The current solution is insufficient so there is currently a gap in the market, which we are looking forward to filling.

Proposed Application

The "Seattle Hangout Guide" is an interactive web application designed to enhance the experience of discovering and planning activities in Seattle. Aimed at residents, tourists, and students, the app provides a user-friendly platform for exploring the city's diverse offerings, from dining to multiple activities like hiking and Axe-throwing. It caters to a broad audience, including families, students, and solo travelers, ensuring inclusivity for minority groups. The app will especially benefit those new to the city or looking to explore unfamiliar neighborhoods, offering a unique tool to enrich their social, cultural experiences, and promote tourism.

Users will interact with the app through features like a personalized itinerary builder, similar to a Google Calendar, where they can select activities based on their interests. The 'Activities Wrapped' feature, similar to Spotify Wrapped, will offer a fun summary of their explored activities over time. For a social touch, users can share itineraries or activity ideas with friends. Additionally, the app includes a feature for users to review activities, enriching the community knowledge base. This interactive approach not only personalizes the user experience but also fosters a sense of community among Seattle explorers.

The app will leverage APIs like FourSquare Places for detailed information on local venues, ensuring up-to-date and relevant content. By offering a centralized platform for discovering and planning activities, the app addresses the challenge of scattered information sources, streamlining the process of finding things to do in Seattle. It's not just an informational tool but an interactive guide that adapts to user preferences, enhancing their ability to explore the city in a personalized way. The use of APIs ensures a dynamic and current data flow, crucial for activities that are time-sensitive or dependent on external factors like weather or special events. In solving the problem of fragmented and overwhelming choices, the "Seattle Hangout Guide" becomes more than just an app – it's a companion for anyone looking to enrich their social and cultural experiences in Seattle. This project, while not driven by business value, showcases practical utility and user-centric design, key elements in the field of Informatics.

List of APIs

  • Google Places API or FourSquare Places
  • OpenWeatherMap API
  • Eventbrite's API or Ticketmaster API for activities
  • Mapbox or Google Maps API
    • Mapbox is generally more generous with its free offerings compared to Google Maps
    • Completely free alternatives - OpenStreetMap with libraries like Leaflet for map integration
    • Yelp Fusion API
